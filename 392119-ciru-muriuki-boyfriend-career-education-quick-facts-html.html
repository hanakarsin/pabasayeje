1. Her father died from COVID-19

Ciru Muriuki recently lost her father to COVID-19. She shared the story on her social media accounts, describing her father as a loving and supporting individual. She wrote:

What life can never prepare you for…is to lose a beloved parent. My most honoured, most cherished father has rested. I have grappled with the unimaginable pain of losing my anchor, my best friend, the best man I have ever known.

Ciru’s father was 71 years old and was the bedrock of the family. He passed away after a one-week stay at the hospital getting treated for COVID-19. Ciru also tested positive for the virus, but her condition wasn’t fatal.

2. Ciru has undergone surgery for endometriosis

Ciru who is currently working for the BBC, underwent her third surgery for endometriosis in 2020. However, she was hospitalized for another surgery to improve her health.

She announced the news on her Instagram page, getting great emotional support from her fans that wished her a speedy recovery.

Hae. So much. So, I'm back in the hospital for my THIRD surgery. This journey with endometriosis has not been easy. On the bright side, my skin is popping. We thank God.

Ciru Muriuki opened up about her struggle with endometriosis in 2017. She was battling the condition in silence for fear of stigmatization.
