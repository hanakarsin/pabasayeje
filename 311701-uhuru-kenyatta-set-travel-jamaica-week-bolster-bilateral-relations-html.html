Uhuru Kenyatta set to travel to Jamaica next week for bilateral relations

Publish date: 2024-04-16

- President Uhuru Kenyatta will jet out on Monday, August 5 for bilateral talks

- The Head of State is expected to attend Jamaican national day celebrations

- The president will visit Bob Marley's museum during his Jamaican tour

President Uhuru Kenyatta and Prime Minister of Jamaica Andrew Holnes will hold bilateral talks when the two leaders meet in Kingston city.

State House confirmed on Tuesday, July 30, the Head of State is set to jet out of the country on Monday, August 5, following invitation by host president.

Talks on enhanced trade between the two nations as well as possibility of having a direct flight from Nairobi to Kingston are expected to feature prominently.

Uhuru is expected to grace and give address during the Caribbean nation's independence day celebrations slated for Wednesday, August 6.

"During the visit, the President and his host Prime Minister Holness together with their respective delegations will discuss and explore opportunities for enhanced bilateral relations between Kenya and Jamaica," State House spokesperson Kanze Dena said.

While in Jamaica, Uhuru will grace and give address during the Caribbean nation's independence day celebrations slated for Tuesday, August 6.

In 2017, Uhuru confessed he loved roots reggae music which is highly associated with late icon Bob Nester Marley whose museum he will visit.

The president will also tour shrine of Jamaican-born pan-Africanist Marcus Mosiah Garvey.
