Uasin Gishu governor Jackson Mandago differs with Ruto over JKIA merger with Kenya Airways

Publish date: 2024-02-17

- The governor argued KQ has been making loses and cannot take over management of JKIA which is stable

- Mandago said it would have only been possible if JKIA was to take over from KQ and not the reverse

- The governor asked Members of Parliament to remain firm and stop the proposed controversial merger

- He appeared to contradict Deputy President William Ruto who had asked MPs to allow the merger

Uasin Gishu governor, Jackson Mandago, has sharply differed with Deputy President William Ruto's plea to Members of Parliament to allow national carrier Kenya Airways (KQ) take over management of Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA).

Mandago argued it was not possible for a KQ take over the management of the airport when the former has been making huge losses from time to time and has always been bailed out by the government.

In a video seen by TUKO.co.ke on Monday, March 4, the North Rift governor said the deal would have only been meaningful had the proposal suggested KAA should take over management of KQ in the proposed merger.

"In the whole world, I have never seen a company that is making losses take over management of a company that is making profit, it is always the reverse. Kenya Airports Authority (KAA) should have taken over the management of Kenya Airways. That would have been acceptable," he said.

The governor also took issues with the management of KQ over the funds the government has been pumping in to bail it out.

He demanded to know how the billions were used given the company was still struggling financially.

"Kenya Airways must first tell us how they have been spending bail out money that has always been issued to the company by the government for many years now," said Mandago

He vowed to frustrate the efforts for the merger and urged MPs to remain firm and steadfast to reject the proposal.

"As the governor whose county has the third largest airport in the country, I will not allow that and MPs should reject that proposal completely," he added

Ruto had endorsed the proposed handing over of JKIA to KQ arguing the merger would help resuscitate the cash-strapped KQ.

However, Parliament's Public Investments Committee (PIC) and Transport Committee members vowed to block the merger as they also questioned how a financially crippled company would manage a stable entity.
