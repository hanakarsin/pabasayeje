Police officers in Kisii are pursuing suspects who set ablaze six churches in Otamba area Nyaribari Chache constituency.

Kisii county commander Jebel Munene revealed security had been beefed up in the region with 24-hour patrols set up by his team in an effort to nab the criminals and restore order.

"We have had a series of meetings with all security stakeholders and in the coming days, we are likely to arrest the main suspects. We now have 24-hour security patrols in the area," Munene was quoted by The Standard.

His remarks came after St Augustine Catholic Church in the area was torched by unknown people a week ago leaving its pulpit completely destroyed.

Two houses belonging to church members were also set ablaze sparking uproar from residents and the leadership of the church.

Abuse of drugs

The bizzare behaviour was linked to abuse of drugs and idle school dropouts.

On Saturday, January 23, night, similar incidents were witnessed after Otamba Pentecostal Assemblies of God (PAG) church, Nyangeni Seventh Day Adventist (SDA) and Otamba Worldwide church which were also razed down.

At the same time, several shops at Menyinkwa near Nyaura Secondary School were also burnt.

Julius Nyaribo, a member of Nyumba Kumi community policing, said the actions amounted to a curse that would be severely punished by God.

"The burning of churches is a curse to all those who committed the crime. God will do wonders and have them surrender to the authorities." he said.

He urged police officers to take swift action and carry out detailed investigations so as to bring the culprits to book.

Three prosperity gospel churches razed down

In May 2020, a Ugandan man burnt down three prosperity gospel churches for allegedly not helping him out of poverty.

Reagan John Ngobi raided the places of worship armed with three brand new knives and doused the properties with petrol before putting them on fire.

Detectives said the 21-year-old was annoyed by the prosperity gospel the churches preached everyday yet he remained poor.

Among things reduced to ashes included clothes belonging to a reverend, church books, altar wine, curtains, a sofa set and plastic chairs.
