Ugandan President Yoweri Museveni has been sworn in office for a sixth term with the promise of upholding democracy.

Speaking at the Kololo Ceremonial Grounds in Kampala on Wednesday, May 12, Museveni observed that Uganda was a democratic country that doesn't need any validation from external forces.

Museveni said that all those in attendance had taken COVID-19 tests even as he assured them of their safety.

At least 4,000 guests were invited to attend the ceremony under strict COVID-19 prevention measures.

President Uhuru Kenyatta, who doubles as the chairman of the East African Community, was among African leaders who attended the swearing-in ceremony.

"I find it quite comic and laughable to hear some actors in the world giving us a lecture about democracy. You give me a lecture about democracy. What are your own credentials?" posed Museveni.

The 76-year-old said that his ruling National Resistance Movement (NRM) designed the current system in Uganda after a long period of the bush war in the central region.

"This current system was designed not from the air-conditioned rooms but from the jungles of our country where we lived with the people in their huts those days for much of the 16 years of the resistance, 1971-1986," Museveni said.

Museveni was involved in rebellions that toppled Uganda leaders Idi Amin (1971-1979) and Milton Obote (1980-1985) before he captured power in 1986.

He observed that what is needed in the East African nation is to expunge the corruption of money that he said has been creeping in.

"These are monies brought in by the opportunist careerists looking for jobs and not for service," added Museveni.

Museveni termed as laughable efforts by some external forces who have been trying to lecture his administration about democracy.

"We build this system for our people. We neither seek nor need any approbation from anyone outside Uganda except our peers in the African Union and the African Union Development Agency-NEPAD to whom we are linked by African brotherhood, solidarity and shared values based on mutual respect," Museveni explained.

At the same time, Museveni said that apart from importing COVID-19 vaccines, his government is developing its own jabs that will deal with all the coronavirus disease variants.

"We shall import once or twice then make our own. We were delayed because of our slave-minded civil servants who have no confidence in themselves," said Museveni.

In the January 14, 2021 polls, Museveni's main challenger, Robert Kyagulanyi Ssentamu alias Bobi Wine, said earlier that Ugandan security forces had confined him to house arrest ahead of the inauguration.
