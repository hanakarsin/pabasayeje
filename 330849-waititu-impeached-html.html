Kiambu: Governor Ferdinand Waititu impeached

Publish date: 2024-07-26

- At least 63 out of 92 MCAs voted to remove Ferdinand Waititu from office

- The ward representatives accused the troubled governor of gross misconduct and abuse of office

- The politician, his wife and 11 others were accused of defrauding the county KSh KSh 588,198,328 and have already been charged in court

- The former Embakasi MP will now face the Senate to defend himself against a litany of accusations

A majority of Kiambu county assembly members have voted to remove Governor Ferdinand Waititu from office.

A total 63 MCAs approved the impeachment motion to send the governor home following a heated debate that was conducted on the afternoon of Thursday, November 19.

Ndenderu MCA Solomon Kinuthia tabled the motion which could potentially obliterate the political career of the controversial embattled former Embakasi legislator.

The House has 92 elected and nominated ward representatives but 28 MCAs did not turn up for the session while only one voted to save the county boss.

The die is not yet cast as Waititu will have to appear before the Senate to defend himself against a litany of accusations, key among them gross misconduct and abuse of office.

Currently, the politician who weaved his way into the heart of Kenyan politics from a councilor to represent two constituencies in Nairobi and Kiambu counties, is fighting to clear his name of graft allegations.

Waititu, his wife Susan and 12 individuals among them six Kiambu government executive committee members were jointly accused of defrauding the county KSh 588,198,328.

The amount was meant for the upgrade of various roads to bituminous surface in the 2017/2018 financial year.

According to the Office of the Director of Public Prosecution (ODPP), the specific award was skewed in favour of M/S Testimony Enterprises Ltd whose directors are believed to be the governor's close associates despite county officials' knowledge.

Out the total amount, at least KSh 147,274,055 was irregularly paid to M/S Testimony Enterprises Ltd.

In addition to the subject tender, the contractor received other contracts which included supply of goods worth KSh 74, 216, 444, already wired to the contractor's account.

In total, the ODPP held that Testimony Enterprises Ltd pocketed KSh 221, 490, 499 out of which KSh 25, 624, 500 was remitted to entities associated with Waititu.

Part of the funds was used by Saika Two Estate Developers to purchase Bienventure Delta Hotel owned by the county chief and his wife.

The politician is listed as the owners of Saika Two Estate Developers.

He was arrested and later produced at Milimani Law Courts on Tuesday, July 30, where Justice Lawrence Mugambi ruled he should never step in the county office until the case is determined.

His deputy James Nyoro took charge of the county affairs on Tuesday, July 30, and promised to seal corruption loopholes.

The two do not see each other eye to eye.
