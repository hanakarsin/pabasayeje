Kisumu 100: Lakeside City Continues to Record Highest Number of New COVID-19 Cases

Publish date: 0001-01-01

- Kisumu county has recorded 100 new cases of COVID-19 followed by Nairobi with 91 cases

- The surge in Kisumu's COVI-19 cases comes a week after Maradaka Day celebrations were held there

- The new infections push the tally of confirmed positive cases in the country to 173,661 and the cumulative number of tests conducted to 1,845,884

COVID-19 infections in Kisumu county have continued to rise a week after the 58th Madaraka Day celebrations were held in the lakeside city.

In a statement issued by the Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe, the county recorded 100 new cases of COVID-19 followed by Nairobi County with 91.

The CS announced that the country reported 589 new confirmed cases from 4,995 samples tested in the last 24 hours.

Other western counties Bungoma recording (60), Homa Bay (54) and Siaya (49), Uasin Gishu had (37) new confirmed cases, Busia (32), Nandi (24), Mombasa (17), Nakuru (17), Kakamega (12), Nyeri (11), Migori( 11), Vihiga (nine), Kiambu (eight), Bomet (seven), Meru (seven), Kericho (six), Kilifi (six), Kisii (five), Elgeyo.

"Marakwet recored (five), Murang’a (four), Kajiado (three), West Pokot (two), Nyandarua (two), Samburu (one), Taita Taveta (one), Trans Nzoia (one), Turkana (one,) Embu (one), Laikipia (one), Machakos (one), Narok (one), Nyamira (one) and Garissa (one)," the CS stated.

As of Wednesday, June 9, the country's COVID-19 positivity rate stood at 11.8 %, an increase compared to 9.7 % on Tuesday, June 8.

The new infections push the total of confirmed positive cases in the country to 173,661 and the cumulative number of tests conducted to 1,845,884.

The new infections comprise 580 Kenyans and nine foreigners, while 343 are male while 246 are female.

The youngest patient is a five-month-old infant, while the oldest is 101 years old.

Kagwe said 19 more deaths were recorded, raising the country’s death toll to 3,345. The CS nonetheless clarified that the deaths were all late reports from April and May, confirmed after audit of data from health facilities.

The ministry further reported 312 more recoveries; 184 of them being registered under the home-based care programme, while 128 being discharged from hospitals.

This pushed the number of recoveries since April 2020 to 118,933.

On Tuesday, June 8, TUKO.co.ke reported that severe symptoms of COVID-19 were reported in Kisumu City.

In the past few days, the lakeside city has recorded a surge in the number of infections after both the Indian and British variants were confirmed.

The infections spread rapidly after politicians held huge rallies before and after the Madaraka Day celebrations.
