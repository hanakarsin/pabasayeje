New survey ranks Safaricom best company to work for in Kenya

Publish date: 2024-07-31

- It is the second time Safaricom is grabbing the top position as the best company to work for in Kenya

- East Africa Breweries Limited came in second followed by United Nations, Kenya Commercial Bank, Kenya Revenue Authority, Kenya Pipeline Company and KenGen

- The telco was earlier listed Africa's best employer by Forbes, clinching position 67 in a list comprised of 200 global companies

Kenya's most profitable telecommunication giant Safaricom has been named as the best company to work for in a freshly released report.

The study was conducted by recruitment agency, BrighterMonday Kenya and was made public in Nairobi on Tuesday, October 22.

The survey saw East Africa Breweries (EABL) grab the second position as the best company to work for followed by the United Nations (UN).

In the fourth place was Kenya Commercial Bank (KCB) while Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) was listed in the fifth position followed by Kenya Pipeline Company which clinched the sixth spot.

Other firms that made it to the top 10 list were KenGen, Price Water Housecoopers (PWC), Cocacola and national carrier Kenya Airways (KQ).

Respondents who were questioned during the study said among preferred intrinsic traits they looked for while seeking employment was a competitive pay package followed by job security.

"Competitive pay package was the top most ranked intrinsic trait, followed by job security, career growth, financially stable company and welfare benefits," BrighterMonday CEO Emmanuel Mutuma said during unveiling of the study.

Respondents also said flexible hours, strong relatable company values and goals, open and effective management, health programs, transparent performance management and mentoring and coaching were key extrinsic factors they examined.

Others points were experimentational environment, family support, diversity hiring and training.

"Respondents aged between 25-35 are the happiest age group at work, followed by 18-24; however, both age groups posed a high flight risk. The oldest age group is the least happy group but not willing to leave their current employer. In Kenya, employees are most satisfied when they can relate to a company’s vision, have full transparency with management and have flexibility in the execution of their tasks,” said Mutuma.
