List of Key Government Projects Rolled Out In Once-Neglected Ukambani Region

The lower eastern region of the country has for long grappled with challenges such as water shortage and a poor road network.

But it has, over the last nine years, benefited from various multi-billion shilling government projects.

They include the KSh 82 billion Thwake dam, Konza Technopolis and the Kibwezi-Mutomo-Kitui-Migwani road.

For the past nine years, President Uhuru Kenyatta has been in office, and the country has witnessed a massive roll-out of infrastructural development projects.

Interestingly, some of the regions that have benefitted greatly from the development agenda are traditionally considered opposition strongholds.

Last week, the head of state visited Ukambani, where Uhuru inspected a number of projects that his government is implementing in the semi-arid region that has for long grappled with challenges such as water shortage and a poor road network.

Below are some of the multi-billion shilling government projects that have been rolled out across the three Ukambani counties.

1.Thwake multi-purpose dam

The KSh 82 billion dam is a project under the Tanathi Water Services Board (TAWSB). It is one of the most expensive landmark projects the Jubilee administration has launched in Ukambani.

Upon completion, the dam is expected to save the region from the nagging water scarcity crisis that has hit the region for decades.

At least 1.3 million residents of Makueni, Kitui and parts of Machakos County are expected to benefit from this project.

The government also intends to establish an irrigation scheme to irrigate 40,000 hectares of land.

2. Konza Technopolis

This is one of the milestone projects being pushed by the government in line with Kenya’s Vision 2030 economic development blueprint.

Upon its completion, Konza is expected to be a world-class city, powered by a thriving Information, Communications and Technology (ICT) sector, superior, reliable infrastructure and business-friendly governance systems.

The city, which sits on a 5 000-acre piece of land cutting across Machakos, Makueni and Kajiado counties, will also have a science park, a convention centre, shopping malls, hotels, international schools, and a health facility.

Konza city is being constructed in four phases and is estimated to cost the government about KSh 1.2 trillion.

The first phase alone will create direct employment for over 20 000 people.

3. Yatta Canal Rehabilitation

The rehabilitation of the Yatta Canal and Water Supply System, which is complete, was funded by the Africa Development Bank and the government of Kenya at the cost of KSh 2.2 billion.

The project currently serves 5,000 farmers with irrigation water and 75,000 residents with clean water.

4. Yatta Dam and Yatta Water Supply Project

The designs for the project that is expected to cost KSh 8.3 billion to be complete. The components of the project include the construction of a 30metre high dam to supply water to Matuu, Sofia and Katangi towns and their environs.

5. Machakos Water Supply

The Machakos Water supply and Sanitation Project is being constructed at the cost of KSh 2.7 billion.

The projected daily production capacity will be 15,000m3. The project components include constructing a water supply system, construction of 15metre-high Miwongoni dam, rehabilitation of Maruba dam and the existing water supply system, and construction of 5,000m3 tank and 3,000m3 tanks.

6. Kibwezi-Mutomo-Kitui-Migwani road

The 192km road, which is part of the Mombasa-Isiolo-Addis Ababa Transport Corridor, directly links Mombasa and Ethiopia through Isiolo and Moyale, thus opening the lower Eastern region for trade.

It has also opened up Kibwezi, Ikutha, Mutomo and Kitui townships for business and eased traffic along the busy Mombasa-Nairobi highway.

7. Tala-Kangundo road

The construction of Tala-Kangundo-Koma Road is another mega project the government has initiated to open up and connect the lower Eastern region to Nairobi and other parts of the country. The project is estimated to cost KSh 4.4 billion.

Other key Roads

The government is also constructing the Sultan Hamud Kasikeu-Wautu-Kyambeke-Kikoko road at the cost of KSh 1.4 billion.

In addition, about KSh 587 million has been allocated to construct the Athi River Axle Load Control Station.

Other ongoing projects include upgrading to Bitumen Standards of Machakos TTC Road, upgrading of Syokimau-Katani Phase, routine maintenance of Lot 17; Katumani - Vota - Konza road.

Through the World Bank-Kenya Transport Sector Support Project, the government is also undertaking the Athi River - Machakos Phase 1 road at the cost of KSh 5.7 billion.
