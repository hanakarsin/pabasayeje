Pascal Tokodi hysterical after spotting Uhuru taking stroll outside State House: &quot;Ona Selina&quot;

Publish date: 2024-04-07

- Pascal Tokodi was driving around State House Road when he pumped into the president taking a stroll

- The stunned actor immediately greeted Uhuru before reminding him that he's a thespian

- The president was very cool and kind enough to respond and even promised to watch the TV show

How would you react if your day went from driving around handling your errands to what could be your first-ever meeting with the president?

This appears to be the identical thing that happened to actor Pascal Tokodi who bumped into the president during a drive along State House Road in the company of a friend on Wednesday, February 24.

The 27-year-old could not believe his eyes when he spotted the 4th during what appeared to be his casual afternoon walk.

He immediately took out his phone, began recording the unbelievable moment and greeted him in the most star-struck way imaginable.

"How are you sir?" Oh my goodness!" Tokodi shouted hysterically.

At this time, his friend, seated on the passengers' seat, gave out a huge smile while waving the V-sign at the president like a rapper waving to his fans at the entrance of a concert.

Tokodi, still having probably the best day of his year yet, wished the president a good day but before he drove off, decided it was a good idea to remind Uhuru that he's actually a celebrity, just like him.

"Ona Selina!" Ona Selina!" He could be heard shouting, urging the president to tune in to the next episode of Selina drama TV show, where he plays the role of starring, no villain, no wait - starring... you will just have to watch it to understand.

The president was kind enough to wave back and even appeared to note that he had actually watched Selina or was it that he was going to?

Nevertheless, Tokodi's day, week, month or even year was made and he drove off to boast about his crazy day which we assume wife Grace Ekirapa has listened to over a gazillion times, lol!

But what a day for Tokodi.

Well, the focus turns to what Uhuru was doing walking all alone outside the house on the hill and if his security detail was just nearby watching in silence.

And his stick, what a beautiful piece of furniture that is. It screamed authority all the way.

Was it a victory lap after nearly all counties gave BBI a pass in what Raila Odinga termed a 'Super Tuesday'?

Speaking of Raila, his celebration on the same occasion involved a tweet about his love for the song Reggae Music Again by Busy Signal which he, apparently, woke up listening to.

In a move that also proved the BBI Reggae is not going away anytime soon, the Jamaican singer came across the tweet and showed his appreciation through emojis.

This ended a lovely day where political personalities met art celebrities on totally different avenues. What a day!
