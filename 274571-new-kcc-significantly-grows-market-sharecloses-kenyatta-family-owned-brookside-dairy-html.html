New KCC significantly grows market share,closes in on Kenyatta family-owned Brookside dairy

Publish date: 2024-07-11

- Milk production across the country has increased owing to the ongoing heavy rains

- Brookside dairies control the country's dairy market with a 40% market share

- New KCC has grown it's share of the market to 35% from 23% same time 2016

Following heavy rains pounding across the country, milk production has significantly increased raising market share of raw milk consumption by dairy companies.

New Kenya Co-operatives Creameries (KCC) increased sharply its share of raw milk intake by 2% to close in on Kenyatta family affiliated Brookside dairy.

Data by the Kenya Dairy Board 2017 indicated the state-owned dairy cemented its second position with a commanded of 35% of the raw milk uptake up from 23% in 2016.

Brookside remained the market leader with 40% of the market, a position it has held for years now.

Despite the acquisition of other milk processors between 2007 and 2013, the leading dairy has been conceding market share.

The acquisition of Buzeki Dairy, which was the last in it's list of acquisitions in 2013, had pushed market share of the Ruiru-based dairy to 44%.

Githunguri Dairy Farmers Cooperative comes in third with a market share of 10%, having shed off 6% points in the last three years.

New KCC, whose market share has risen steadily since 2016, has been expanding its plant to accommodate more milk.
