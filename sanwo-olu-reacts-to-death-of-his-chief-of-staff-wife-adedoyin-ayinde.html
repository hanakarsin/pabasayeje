Sanwo-Olu Reacts To Death Of His Chief Of Staff Wife, Adedoyin Ayinde

Publish date: 2024-04-27

The Lagos State Governor, Babajide Sanwo-Olu, has reacted to the death of his Chief of staff’s wife, Adedoyin Ayinde, amidst the reports that she allegedly committed suicide.

Naija News earlier reported that Ayinde lost his wife to the cold hands of death on Sunday morning, 13 November in Lagos, just 24 hours after she returned from London where she had gone to birth her second child.

However, there are reports making the rounds online that Adedoyin allegedly committed suicide over her husband’s relationship with his secretary at the office.

In a statement shared on his social media page, Sanwo-Olu commiserated with the family of the deceased, stating it is a painful death but God can never be questioned.

He noted that Adedoyin impacted many lives positively in the Apapa Local Government Area of the state where she served and contributed to the development of the LG and the state.

The statement reads: “On behalf of my family, my cabinet, the Lagos State government, and the people of Lagos State, I commiserate with my Chief of Staff, Mr. Tayo Ayinde on the death of his beloved wife, Princess Adedoyin Raliat Ayinde, née Ojora.

“No doubt the death of a loved one is usually a painful episode in our lives, because it is irreparable. But we must always take solace in God whom we can never question.

“We should be consoled by the fact that she impacted many lives positively as a public officer at the Apapa Local Government Area, where she served passionately and contributed her quota to the growth and development of the local government and Lagos State.

“Princess Adedoyin Raliat Ayinde was a trusted partner, friend, wife, and great supporter to her husband, Mr. Tayo Ayinde and a caring mother to her children, and a loving daughter to her parents. May God grant all her loved ones the fortitude to bear this irreparable loss.

“Eternal rest grant her O Lord. Amen”
