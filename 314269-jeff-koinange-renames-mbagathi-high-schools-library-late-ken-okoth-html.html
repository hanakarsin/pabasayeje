Jeff Koinange renames Mbagathi High School's library after late Ken Okoth

Publish date: 2024-03-12

Journalist Jeff Koinange has renamed a library he constructed in Mbagathi High School after the late Kibra MP Ken Okoth.

The journalist who had initially named the library Jeff Koinange Library changed the name to Ken Okoth Memorial Library in honour of the late MP.

In a Facebook post he shared on Saturday, August 31, Jeff also stated he had started stacking the library with books in line with the promise he had made to Okoth.

"Folks almost two years ago, former Kibra MP, the late Ken Okoth challenged me on the bench to build a library for the kids at Mbagathi High School. He called it the "Jeff Koinange Library. Today, we have started stacking the shelves with books and on top of that rename the library the Ken OKoth Memorial Library," he stated in the Facebook post.

He called upon friends of the late MP and willing members of the public to also chip in and help stock the library with learning material.

Jeff and Okoth were close friends and were pictured together during the construction of the facility.

The news anchor lauded the former MP for his commitment to uplift the living standards of Kibra residents through initiating developmental projects.

Okoth invested heavily in education and Mbagathi High School towering block is one of his landmark projects he built from scratch using Constituency Development Funds (CDF).

Under his leadership, Kibra constituency was ranked the best in management of CDF in 2015.
