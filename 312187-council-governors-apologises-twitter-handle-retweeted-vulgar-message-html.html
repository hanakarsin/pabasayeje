Council of Governors apologises after its Twitter handle retweeted vulgar message

Publish date: 2024-02-08

The Council of Governors has issued an apology to Kenyans after its Twitter handle retweeted vulgar message.

The council had retweeted a message from one Taruri Gatere who was replying to a Jamaican tweep on August, Monday 5.

The message contained some words that one is likely to hear when people are having sex.

"Say my name. Moan. Tell me how it feels. Tell me what you like. Tell me how much you like it. Ask me questions I can't answer because I'm too into it. Look into my eyes and smile and say hi," read the message that was retweeted.

After the tweet went viral, CoG issued an apology implying they had temporarily lost control of the Twitter handle.

"We sincerely apologise for the inappropriate retweet through our Twitter handle. We have regained control of our handle and have put proper measures to ensure that this does not occur again," read the apology.

The messages have since been deleted from CoG's Twitter page.
